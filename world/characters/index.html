Captain Andreas

Blunt, battle-worn Byzantine officer who fought at the Battle of the Echinades (1427) and helped secure and administer Glarentza for Constantine. Skeptical of innovation at first, he becomes a trusted commander as Constantine’s ideas prove themselves in drill, discipline, and war.

First appearance: Codex entry

Theophilus Dragas

Sent by Helena Dragas to safeguard the new regime in Glarentza, he runs the civilian engine: publishing, treasury, and the wider economy that funds Constantine’s reforms. Tight-fisted and relentless in his accounting, his stinginess is rooted in family loyalty.

First appearance: Codex entry

Theodora Tocco

Daughter of Carlo I Tocco and Constantine’s first wife in a hard-edged political marriage after Echinades (1427). The union stabilizes the Morea but ends in tragedy when she dies in [YEAR] during childbirth, leaving Constantine permanently changed.

First appearance: Codex entry

Georgios Gemistos Plethon

The famed, elderly philosopher of Mystras who relocates to Glarentza, becoming an intellectual weapon for reform. He co-architects Ieros Skopos with Constantine, shaping it into a manifesto meant to ignite identity, resistance, and purpose.

First appearance: Codex entry

Iskander

Tatar scholar and revolutionary, once a follower of Sheikh Bedreddin, found among prisoners near Corinth. Freed and weaponized by Constantine, he crafts manifestos and secret networks that stir the oppressed under the promise of a world “without masters.”

First appearance: Codex entry

Bessarion

Constantine’s bridge to the West, a monk-philosopher and protégé of Plethon who moves through Rome, Naples, and Florence. He brokers alliances, sells books, and turns Hellenic prestige into gold and influence while weighing Church union as necessary.

First appearance: Codex entry

Theodore II Palaiologos

Constantine’s chief domestic antagonist: devout, austere, and resentful of innovation. He leads the anti-unionist faction from obstruction to sabotage, culminating in a palace coup with Demetrios that kills John VIII before Theodore is betrayed and slain.

First appearance: Codex entry

Emperor John VIII Palaiologos

The weary Basileus in Constantinople, balancing diplomacy and collapsing walls while trying to contain rival brothers. A pragmatic unionist, he is assassinated in a late-1432 palace coup orchestrated from within the family.

First appearance: Codex entry

Francesco Sforza

Italy’s most formidable condottiero, hired at staggering cost. He recognizes Constantine’s new combined-arms doctrine, holds the Hexamilion with brutal discipline, and departs inspired to carry these methods back to Italy.

First appearance: Codex entry

Sultan Murad II

Ottoman Sultan and primary external antagonist, seasoned and calculating. Murad treats Byzantium’s resurgence as an imperial insult, weighing caution against pride as he prepares decisive punishment.

First appearance: Codex entry
